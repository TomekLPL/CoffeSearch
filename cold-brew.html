ColdBrew

About this exquisite coffee:

Cold brew coffee is a refreshing and smooth coffee beverage made by steeping coarsely ground coffee beans in cold or room temperature water for an extended period, usually 12 to 24 hours. The slow extraction process produces a concentrated coffee concentrate that is later diluted with water or milk before serving.

Unlike traditional hot-brewed coffee, cold brew is known for its lower acidity and milder, less bitter flavor profile. It often has subtle, nuanced undertones, making it a popular choice for those who enjoy a smoother and less intense coffee experience.

Cold brew can be served over ice and is often customizable with sweeteners, flavored syrups, or cream, depending on individual taste preferences. Its popularity has grown in recent years, and it is commonly found in coffee shops and easily prepared at home using various brewing devices designed specifically for cold brewing.
