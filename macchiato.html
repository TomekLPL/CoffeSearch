Macchiato

About this exquisite coffee:

A macchiato is a coffee beverage that consists of a shot of espresso "stained" or "marked" with a small amount of frothed milk. The term "macchiato" is Italian for "stained" or "spotted," indicating the brief addition of milk to the espresso. This results in a coffee with a strong and bold flavor, complemented by a touch of creaminess from the frothed milk.

There are variations of the macchiato, with one popular version being the "caramel macchiato," where caramel syrup is often added for sweetness. The traditional macchiato, however, emphasizes the pure and robust taste of the espresso with just a hint of milk.

Macchiatos are often served in smaller cups, and their simplicity highlights the quality of the coffee. The balance between the intensity of the espresso and the light touch of milk makes the macchiato a favorite for those who enjoy a strong coffee experience with a subtle creaminess.
