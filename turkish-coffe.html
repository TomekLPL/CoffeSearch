About this exquisite coffee:

Turkish coffee is a traditional method of coffee preparation that originated in the Middle East and is closely associated with Turkish and Arabic cultures. It involves brewing finely ground coffee beans with water and sugar (optional) in a special pot called a "cezve" or "ibrik."

To make Turkish coffee, finely ground coffee, water, and sugar (if desired) are combined in the cezve and slowly heated over low heat. The coffee is not filtered, and the fine grounds settle at the bottom of the cup. Turkish coffee is known for its strong and robust flavor, as well as its distinctive preparation and serving style.

Traditionally, Turkish coffee is served in small cups without milk. It is often accompanied by a glass of water to cleanse the palate. The preparation and serving of Turkish coffee hold cultural significance, and the beverage is often enjoyed as part of social and cultural rituals.
