About this exquisite coffee:

Cuban coffee, also known as "cafecito" or "Cuban espresso," is a strong and sweet coffee preparation that is popular in Cuba and among coffee enthusiasts worldwide. It is typically made using a stovetop espresso maker called a "cafetera" or an espresso machine.

The key element of Cuban coffee is the addition of sugar to the coffee grounds during the brewing process, resulting in a sweet and concentrated espresso. The coffee is often served in small cups known as "demitasse" cups. Cuban coffee is deeply ingrained in Cuban culture, often enjoyed as a social ritual and a pick-me-up throughout the day. It reflects the influence of Spanish and Latin American coffee traditions while incorporating a distinct Cuban flair.
