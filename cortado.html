Cortado

About this exquisite coffee:

A cortado is a coffee beverage that originated in Spain and is characterized by a simple and balanced combination of equal parts espresso and warm milk. The word "cortado" translates to "cut" in Spanish, referring to the practice of "cutting" the espresso with a small amount of steamed milk.

Typically served in a small glass, a cortado maintains the strong and robust flavor of espresso while reducing its intensity by incorporating the smoothness of steamed milk. The result is a drink that strikes a balance between the boldness of espresso and the creaminess of milk, making it a popular choice for those who enjoy a well-rounded coffee experience without the heaviness of a larger milk-based drink.

Cortados are often served without additional flavorings or sweeteners, allowing the natural flavors of the coffee to shine through. The precise ratios of espresso to milk can vary, but the essence of a cortado lies in its harmonious blend of these two key components.
