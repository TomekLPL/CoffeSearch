FlatWhite

About this exquisite coffee:

A flat white is a coffee beverage that originated in Australia or New Zealand and has gained popularity worldwide. It is made by pouring microfoam (steamed milk with fine, velvety bubbles) over a shot of espresso. The key feature of a flat white is the balance between the rich intensity of espresso and the creamy texture of the microfoam.

Unlike a latte, which has more steamed milk and a thin layer of foam, or a cappuccino, which has equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam, a flat white has a higher coffee-to-milk ratio and a thin layer of microfoam. This results in a strong and smooth coffee with a velvety mouthfeel.

The flat white has become a popular choice in many coffee shops, known for its simple yet sophisticated composition that highlights the quality of the espresso. It is often served in a smaller cup than a latte, making it a favorite for those who enjoy a strong coffee flavor with a touch of creamy texture.
